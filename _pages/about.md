Welcome! I am a PhD Candidate in Systems & Controls at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, specializing in data-driven decision-making, control, and optimization for connected and automated vehicles (CAVs). My research lies at the intersection of intelligent transportation systems, robotics, and reinforcement learning, with a focus on developing safe and efficient control strategies for autonomous driving in complex environments.

I earned my Master of Science in Soft Computing and Intelligent Systems from [Universiti Malaysia Pahang (UMP)](https://www.umpsa.edu.my/en), where I received the 1st Place Winner Excellent Publication Award and was funded through the Ministry of Higher Education’s Master Research Scheme. My undergraduate degree is in Statistics from [Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST)](https://www.sust.edu/),Bangladesh.

My work has been published in leading journals such as Control Engineering Practice, Scientific Reports (Nature), IEEE Access, and Future Generation Computer Systems, among others. I have authored or co-authored 28 peer-reviewed publications, with 13 in Q1 journals and over 650 citations to date. My research has been recognized with multiple awards, including a Gold Medal at the Malaysia Technology Expo and a Special Award from Euro Business Haller (Poland).

I have extensive experience with simulation platforms such as CARLA, Unity3D, and MATLAB Simulink, and I am proficient in Python, C++, and machine learning frameworks like TensorFlow and PyTorch. I currently serve as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Autonomous Systems Laboratory at UTK and actively review for journals including IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Vehicles.
